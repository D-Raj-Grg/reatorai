## What are Watchlists?

**Watchlists** are custom collections of YouTube channels grouped by your criteria. Think of them as playlists for channel research.

### Why Use Watchlists?

**Benefits:**
- **Organization**: Group channels by topic, niche, or purpose
- **Focus**: Filter videos to specific content areas
- **Efficiency**: Quick access to relevant inspiration
- **Strategy**: Separate competitors from aspirational creators
- **Workflow**: Different lists for different research phases

**Example use cases:**
- Track direct competitors separately
- Group channels by content style
- Organize by research priority
- Separate active vs. archived channels

## Creating Watchlists

### Create Your First Watchlist

**Steps:**
1. Navigate to "Watchlists" in sidebar
2. Click "Create Watchlist" button
3. Enter watchlist details:
   - **Name**: Clear, descriptive title
   - **Description**: Purpose or criteria (optional)
   - **Icon**: Choose emoji or icon (optional)
4. Click "Create"
5. Start adding channels

**Quick create from channel:**
- Click "Add to Watchlist" on any channel card
- Select "Create New Watchlist"
- Add channel and create list in one step

### Naming Best Practices

**Good names:**
- "Fitness - Direct Competitors"
- "Tech Reviews - Inspiration"
- "Comedy - Study Later"
- "Finance - High Performers"

**Avoid:**
- Generic names: "List 1", "Channels"
- Too broad: "YouTube"
- Too vague: "Good Ones"

### Adding Channels to Watchlists

**Method 1: From Channels Page**
1. Go to Channels
2. Click "Add to Watchlist" on channel card
3. Select watchlist(s)
4. Confirm

**Method 2: From Watchlist Detail**
1. Open a watchlist
2. Click "Add Channels"
3. Select multiple channels
4. Click "Add Selected"

**Method 3: Bulk Import (Pro Plan)**
1. Open watchlist
2. Click "Bulk Import"
3. Upload CSV or paste channel IDs
4. Review and confirm

## Managing Watchlists

### Viewing Watchlists

**Watchlists page shows:**
- All your watchlists
- Channel count per list
- Last updated date
- Quick actions (edit, delete, view)

**Grid view vs. List view:**
- Grid: Visual overview, see all at once
- List: Detailed info, better for many watchlists

### Editing Watchlists

**Edit watchlist details:**
1. Click "..." menu on watchlist card
2. Select "Edit"
3. Update name, description, icon
4. Save changes

**Reorder channels:**
1. Open watchlist
2. Drag and drop channels (if reorder enabled)
3. Or use sort options

### Removing Channels

**Remove from watchlist:**
1. Open watchlist
2. Click "..." on channel card
3. Select "Remove from Watchlist"
4. Confirm

**Note**: This only removes from watchlist, not from your account

### Deleting Watchlists

**Delete entire watchlist:**
1. Click "..." menu on watchlist card
2. Select "Delete Watchlist"
3. Confirm deletion

**Note**: Channels remain in your account, only the watchlist is deleted

### Sharing Watchlists (Pro Plan)

**Share with team:**
1. Open watchlist
2. Click "Share"
3. Enter team member emails
4. Set permissions (view/edit)
5. Send invitation

**Public sharing:**
- Generate public link
- Anyone with link can view
- Import to their own account
- Track views (Enterprise)

## Organization Strategies

### By Niche/Topic

Group channels by content category:

**Examples:**
- Fitness & Health
- Personal Finance
- Tech & Gadgets
- Cooking & Recipes
- Business & Entrepreneurship
- Gaming & Entertainment

**When to use**: You create content in multiple niches

---

### By Competition Level

Organize by relationship to your content:

**Categories:**
- **Direct Competitors**: Same niche, same size, same audience
- **Adjacent Competitors**: Related niche, crossover appeal
- **Aspirational**: Where you want to be in 1-2 years
- **Industry Leaders**: Top performers to learn from

**When to use**: Competitive analysis, growth planning

---

### By Content Style

Group by how they create content:

**Categories:**
- **Educational**: Tutorials, how-tos, explanations
- **Entertainment**: Comedy, sketches, storytelling
- **Lifestyle**: Vlogs, day-in-life, personal
- **Documentary**: In-depth, investigative
- **Inspirational**: Motivational, aspirational

**When to use**: Learning different content approaches

---

### By Performance Level

Organize by channel size/performance:

**Categories:**
- **Small (0-10k)**: Early-stage, relatable growth tactics
- **Medium (10k-100k)**: Scaling strategies, consistency
- **Large (100k-1M)**: Optimization, systems, team
- **Mega (1M+)**: Enterprise strategies, trends

**When to use**: Learning stage-appropriate tactics

---

### By Research Phase

Organize by how you use them:

**Categories:**
- **Active Study**: Currently analyzing regularly
- **Inspiration**: Check occasionally for ideas
- **Monitoring**: Keep eye on competitors
- **Archive**: Past inspiration, reference only

**When to use**: Managing research workflow

---

### By Upload Frequency

Group by how often they post:

**Categories:**
- **Daily**: High-volume creators
- **Weekly**: Consistent schedule
- **Bi-weekly**: Less frequent
- **Monthly**: Long-form, high-production

**When to use**: Understanding content velocity strategies

---

### By Outlier Rate

Organize by consistency:

**Categories:**
- **Consistently Viral (50%+ outliers)**: Proven systems
- **Occasionally Viral (25-50%)**: Hit-or-miss patterns
- **Rarely Viral (<25%)**: Steady but not spectacular
- **New/Unknown**: Still calculating baseline

**When to use**: Finding reliable pattern sources

## Best Practices

### Start Simple

**Week 1-2: Basic Setup**
- Create 3-5 watchlists max
- Focus on clear categories
- Don't over-organize initially

**Week 3-4: Refine**
- Adjust based on usage
- Combine or split lists as needed
- Add more specific categories

**Month 2+: Optimize**
- Build sophisticated system
- Add advanced categories
- Streamline workflow

### Recommended Starter Watchlists

**For New Creators:**
1. "My Niche - Competitors"
2. "Inspiration - Similar Size"
3. "Study Now - Outliers"

**For Established Creators:**
1. "Direct Competitors"
2. "Adjacent Niches"
3. "Industry Leaders"
4. "Content Styles to Test"

**For Agencies/Teams:**
1. "Client 1 - Niche Research"
2. "Client 2 - Niche Research"
3. "Trend Monitoring"
4. "Competitive Analysis"

### Maintenance Routine

**Weekly:**
- Review each watchlist
- Add new promising channels
- Remove channels no longer relevant
- Check for new outliers per list

**Monthly:**
- Audit entire system
- Consolidate redundant lists
- Create new lists if needed
- Archive inactive lists

### Using Watchlists in Your Workflow

**Content Planning Session:**
1. Open "Active Study" watchlist
2. Filter videos to outliers only
3. Analyze 5-10 recent outliers
4. Generate scripts from patterns
5. Plan content calendar

**Competitive Research:**
1. Open "Competitors" watchlist
2. Sort by recent uploads
3. Check what they're posting
4. Identify gaps in their content
5. Create content to fill gaps

**Inspiration Browsing:**
1. Open "Inspiration" watchlist
2. Scroll through recent outliers
3. Save interesting videos
4. Note patterns and ideas
5. Add to idea backlog

## Troubleshooting

**Q: Can a channel be in multiple watchlists?**
A: Yes! Channels can appear in as many watchlists as you want.

**Q: What happens if I delete a channel that's in watchlists?**
A: It's removed from all watchlists automatically.

**Q: Can I see all videos from a watchlist?**
A: Yes! Click on a watchlist to see all videos from those channels, with filters available.

**Q: How many watchlists can I create?**
A: Free: 5 watchlists, Pro: 25 watchlists, Enterprise: Unlimited

**Q: Can I export a watchlist?**
A: Yes (Pro plan), export as CSV with channel details.
