## Script Generation Basics

### What is Script Generation?

ReatorAI's script generator creates custom short-form content scripts based on analyzed viral videos. The AI:

1. **Analyzes source video**: Extracts proven patterns and techniques
2. **Applies your chosen framework**: Structures content for maximum impact
3. **Customizes for your style**: Matches your tone and voice
4. **Optimizes for platform**: Tailored for YouTube Shorts, TikTok, or Reels
5. **Generates complete script**: Hook, body, CTA, and timing notes

### When to Generate Scripts

**Ideal times:**
- After analyzing multiple outlier videos
- When you've identified a winning pattern
- For batch content creation
- When you have a topic but need structure
- To test different approaches

### Script Generation Process

**Quick Start (5 minutes):**
1. Find an outlier video you want to emulate
2. Click "Analyze Video" (if not already done)
3. Click "Generate Script"
4. Choose hook format and framework
5. Customize style preferences
6. Generate and review
7. Copy or download

## Hook Formats Explained

The **hook** is the first 3 seconds of your video - the most critical moment for retention. Choose from 9 proven formats:

### 1. Question Hook

**What it is**: Open with an intriguing question that creates curiosity

**When to use:**
- Educational content
- Problem-solving videos
- Thought leadership
- Advice content

**Examples:**
- "Ever wonder why some videos go viral and others flop?"
- "What if I told you there's a formula for virality?"
- "Are you making these 3 mistakes with your content?"

**Psychological trigger**: Curiosity gap, engagement, challenges assumptions

**Best for**: Educational niches, how-to content, explainers

---

### 2. Bold Statement Hook

**What it is**: Make a shocking or counterintuitive claim

**When to use:**
- Contrarian content
- Myth-busting
- Industry hot takes
- Attention-grabbing opens

**Examples:**
- "Everything you know about thumbnails is wrong."
- "I made $10,000 in 30 days with this simple hack."
- "This one change tripled my views overnight."

**Psychological trigger**: Shock, surprise, challenges beliefs

**Best for**: Business, finance, productivity, contrarian takes

---

### 3. Personal Story Hook

**What it is**: Start with a relatable personal experience

**When to use:**
- Lifestyle content
- Journey stories
- Transformation content
- Relatable situations

**Examples:**
- "Three months ago, I was exactly where you are now..."
- "This morning, something happened that changed everything..."
- "I'll never forget the day I discovered this..."

**Psychological trigger**: Relatability, emotional connection, empathy

**Best for**: Personal brand, lifestyle, transformation stories

---

### 4. Problem/Solution Hook

**What it is**: Immediately address a pain point

**When to use:**
- Tutorial content
- Product demos
- Life hacks
- Service-based content

**Examples:**
- "Struggling to grow on YouTube? Here's what's holding you back."
- "Tired of wasting hours on scripts? I've got the solution."
- "Can't figure out why your videos aren't getting views?"

**Psychological trigger**: Pain awareness, promise of relief, urgency

**Best for**: SaaS, coaching, education, problem-solving

---

### 5. Curiosity Gap Hook

**What it is**: Create mystery that must be resolved

**When to use:**
- Reveal-style content
- Secret/hack content
- Behind-the-scenes
- Exclusive information

**Examples:**
- "This one thing makes all the difference, and nobody talks about it..."
- "I'm about to show you something that will change your mind..."
- "Wait until you see what happens next..."

**Psychological trigger**: Curiosity, FOMO, anticipation

**Best for**: Entertainment, listicles, reveals, secrets

---

### 6. Numbers/Stats Hook

**What it is**: Lead with impressive data or metrics

**When to use:**
- Data-driven content
- Results showcases
- Case studies
- Credibility building

**Examples:**
- "I analyzed 1,000 viral videos and found these 3 patterns..."
- "89% of creators don't know this one simple trick..."
- "This strategy generated 1 million views in 30 days..."

**Psychological trigger**: Authority, social proof, specificity

**Best for**: Marketing, analytics, case studies, authority content

---

### 7. Before/After Hook

**What it is**: Show transformation to create desire

**When to use:**
- Transformation content
- Progress updates
- Results showcases
- Motivational content

**Examples:**
- "From 0 to 100k subscribers in 6 months - here's exactly how..."
- "Before: Struggling. After: Thriving. The difference? This..."
- "This is what happened when I tried [X] for 30 days..."

**Psychological trigger**: Aspiration, possibility, proof

**Best for**: Fitness, productivity, growth stories, results

---

### 8. Controversy Hook

**What it is**: Take a strong, polarizing stance

**When to use:**
- Opinion content
- Industry commentary
- Trend analysis
- Debate topics

**Examples:**
- "Unpopular opinion: Most content advice is completely wrong."
- "I'm going to say what everyone's thinking but nobody will say..."
- "This is controversial, but someone needs to say it..."

**Psychological trigger**: Agreement/disagreement, engagement, tribalism

**Best for**: Commentary, hot takes, opinion leadership

**Caution**: Can alienate some viewers; use strategically

---

### 9. Direct Address Hook

**What it is**: Speak directly to a specific viewer segment

**When to use:**
- Targeted content
- Niche-specific videos
- Qualification content
- Community building

**Examples:**
- "If you're a small creator struggling to grow, this is for you..."
- "Attention content creators: Stop doing this right now..."
- "This is only for people serious about going viral..."

**Psychological trigger**: Inclusion, identification, targeting

**Best for**: Niche content, community-building, targeted advice

## Frameworks Explained

**Frameworks** structure your entire script for maximum impact. Choose based on your content goal:

### 1. AIDA (Attention, Interest, Desire, Action)

**Best for**: Sales, persuasion, product content

**Structure:**
- **Attention** (0-3s): Hook that stops the scroll
- **Interest** (3-15s): Build intrigue about the topic
- **Desire** (15-45s): Create want/need for solution
- **Action** (45-60s): Clear CTA to take next step

**Use when:**
- Promoting products/services
- Driving specific action
- Sales-focused content

**Example flow:**
1. "Ever feel like your content just disappears?" (Attention)
2. "There's a reason why, and it's not what you think..." (Interest)
3. "Imagine if every video you posted got 10x more views..." (Desire)
4. "Download my free guide to make it happen..." (Action)

---

### 2. PAS (Problem, Agitate, Solution)

**Best for**: Pain-point focused content, problem-solving

**Structure:**
- **Problem** (0-10s): Identify the pain point
- **Agitate** (10-35s): Amplify the problem's impact
- **Solution** (35-60s): Present your solution

**Use when:**
- Addressing specific pain points
- Positioning yourself as solver
- Service/product launches

**Example flow:**
1. "Your videos getting zero views?" (Problem)
2. "Every day that passes, competitors gain ground while you fall behind..." (Agitate)
3. "Here's the 3-step system that changed everything for me..." (Solution)

---

### 3. BAB (Before, After, Bridge)

**Best for**: Transformation stories, results showcases

**Structure:**
- **Before** (0-15s): Show the starting point (relatable struggle)
- **After** (15-30s): Reveal the transformation (aspirational result)
- **Bridge** (30-60s): Explain how to get there (your solution)

**Use when:**
- Sharing transformation stories
- Results-focused content
- Building aspiration

**Example flow:**
1. "6 months ago, I had 127 subscribers..." (Before)
2. "Today, I just hit 100,000..." (After)
3. "Here are the exact 5 things I did to get there..." (Bridge)

---

### 4. PASTOR (Problem, Amplify, Story, Transformation, Offer, Response)

**Best for**: In-depth persuasion, longer-form shorts (60-90s)

**Structure:**
- **Problem** (0-5s): Identify the issue
- **Amplify** (5-15s): Make it urgent/important
- **Story** (15-35s): Share relatable example
- **Transformation** (35-45s): Show what's possible
- **Offer** (45-55s): Present your solution
- **Response** (55-60s): Call to action

**Use when:**
- Complex topics need more context
- Building deep connection
- High-value offers

---

### 5. 4Ps (Problem, Promise, Proof, Proposal)

**Best for**: Educational content with credibility

**Structure:**
- **Problem** (0-10s): What viewer struggles with
- **Promise** (10-20s): What you'll deliver
- **Proof** (20-45s): Evidence it works (data, testimonials, results)
- **Proposal** (45-60s): Next steps to implement

**Use when:**
- Teaching strategies
- Sharing frameworks
- Building authority

---

### 6. The Hero's Journey (3-Act Structure)

**Best for**: Story-driven content, narrative videos

**Structure:**
- **Act 1** (0-20s): Setup - Ordinary world, problem arises
- **Act 2** (20-45s): Conflict - Challenges, attempts, learning
- **Act 3** (45-60s): Resolution - Solution, transformation, lesson

**Use when:**
- Telling stories
- Journey content
- Emotional connection

---

### 7. Hook-Story-Offer (HSO)

**Best for**: Fast-paced, high-energy content

**Structure:**
- **Hook** (0-3s): Grab attention immediately
- **Story** (3-40s): Deliver value through narrative
- **Offer** (40-60s): CTA or next step

**Use when:**
- Fast-paced content
- Entertainment-focused
- Quick value delivery

## Customizing Your Scripts

### Style Preferences

**Tone Options:**
- **Casual**: Conversational, friendly, relatable
- **Professional**: Authoritative, polished, credible
- **Enthusiastic**: Energetic, excited, motivational
- **Educational**: Clear, instructive, helpful

**Length Options:**
- **30 seconds**: Quick hit, single point, fast-paced
- **60 seconds**: Standard short-form, 2-3 points
- **90 seconds**: In-depth, multiple points, storytelling

**Platform Optimization:**
- **YouTube Shorts**: Slightly longer, educational lean
- **TikTok**: Fast-paced, trend-aware, entertaining
- **Instagram Reels**: Visual focus, aesthetic, inspiring

### Advanced Customization (Pro Plan)

**Writing Style:**
- Sentence length preference
- Complexity level
- Vocabulary (simple vs. advanced)
- Humor/seriousness balance

**Personal Voice:**
- Catchphrases to include
- Words to avoid
- Personal quirks
- Brand voice guidelines

**Content Density:**
- Value points per second
- Pacing (fast/medium/slow)
- Depth vs. breadth

## Best Practices

### Script Generation Strategy

**Batch Generation:**
- Generate 5-10 scripts at once
- Different hooks for the same topic
- Multiple frameworks for variety
- Test what resonates

**Iterative Improvement:**
1. Generate initial script
2. Record and test
3. Analyze performance
4. Refine approach
5. Re-generate with adjustments

**Template Building:**
- Save high-performers as favorites
- Identify patterns in winning scripts
- Create personal framework variations
- Build swipe file of hooks

### Making Scripts Your Own

**Always customize:**
- Add personal stories/examples
- Adjust tone to match your voice
- Include your unique insights
- Adapt to current trends

**Don't just read verbatim:**
- Use as framework, not teleprompter
- Natural delivery beats perfect words
- Your personality is the secret sauce
- Scripts are starting points

### Testing Different Approaches

**A/B Test:**
- Same topic, different hooks
- Same framework, different styles
- Same hook, different frameworks
- Track what performs best

**Pattern Recognition:**
- Which hook formats work for your niche?
- Which frameworks resonate with your audience?
- What length performs best?
- Optimal tone for your brand?

## Troubleshooting

**Q: Generated script feels generic**
A: Add more context in customization, use personal stories, adapt to your voice. Scripts are starting points.

**Q: Script doesn't match the video style**
A: Try different framework, adjust tone settings, or manually edit to match desired style.

**Q: Script is too long/short**
A: Adjust length setting, edit manually, or regenerate with different framework.

**Q: Can't generate more scripts**
A: Check your plan limits (20/month free, 100/month Pro), or upgrade plan.
